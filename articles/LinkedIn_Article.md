# Why Your SaaS Launch is Failing: The Technical SEO Mistakes You're Making

When launching a new SaaS product, the primary focus of the product team is typically building features, fixing bugs, and designing user flows. Marketing teams focus on paid ads and PR campaigns. 

However, one of the most critical channels for long-term sustainable growth is often completely ignored: **Technical SEO**.

If your site is not structurally optimized for search engines from day one, you are missing out on the compounding returns of organic acquisition. 

Here are three common technical SEO mistakes that can derail a SaaS launch, and how to fix them.

---

## 1. Relying on Client-Side JavaScript for Key Content
Using standard single-page application (SPA) architectures like classic React or Vue creates a "crawl gap." 

Search engine crawlers download your page, but they cannot immediately index the content because it requires executing complex JavaScript. This can delay your pages appearing in search results by days or weeks.

**The Fix:** Transition your marketing site, blog, and documentation to a server-side rendered (SSR) framework like **Next.js**. Pre-rendering your content on the server sends ready-made HTML to search engines, ensuring your pages are crawled and indexed instantly.

---

## 2. Neglecting Image Alt Text and Core Web Vitals
Slow load times and bad visual stability (Cumulative Layout Shift) are direct ranking penalties in Google's search algorithms. Large, uncompressed images are the most common cause of slow Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) speeds.

**The Fix:** Always use optimized image formats (like WebP or AVIF), compress assets, and declare height/width boundaries to prevent page elements from jumping as they load.

---

## 3. Lacking Google Search Console Integrations
Many startups wait weeks after launch to submit their site for crawling. Google needs active notifications to index pages on new domains.

**The Fix:** Integrate your sitemaps directly with Google Search Console. For large platforms, building a bulk indexing script to notify Google of updates via its Indexing API can accelerate indexing from weeks to minutes.
